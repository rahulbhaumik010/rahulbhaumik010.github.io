Overview

Rahul Bhaumik is a designer and researcher working at the intersection of human-computer interaction, extended reality, soft interfaces, and design pedagogy. Currently, he is a PhD Scholar at the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano, Italy, where his research explores deformable, pressure-sensitive 3D printed structures and novel textile-based interfaces for everyday interactions, including automotive contexts.

Trained as an architect and product designer, Bhaumik's work focuses on the tactile, material, and experiential dimensions of human-technology relationships — drawing upon emerging fabrication technologies and morphological principles to create interfaces that are both functionally innovative and perceptually engaging.

Prior to his doctoral studies, he served as Assistant Professor at the Faculty of Architecture & Design, PES University, Bangalore (2018–2023), where he taught and researched at the confluence of digital design tools, learning systems, and interaction design. He holds an M.Des in Product Design and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, and a B.Arch from the School of Planning and Architecture, New Delhi.
